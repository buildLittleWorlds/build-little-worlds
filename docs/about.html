About

Build Little Worlds is an exploration of how bounded forms help finite minds understand large orders.

The site begins with microcosms: syllables, words, tokens, equations, primes, diagrams, parables, proofs, and model systems. These little worlds are small enough to hold in view, but rich enough to disclose something about language, creation, mathematics, and the Logos.

Theology of LLMs is the first major exploration. Large language models begin with tiny units and local operations, then gain unexpected power through scale. They give the site a modern case study for the back-and-forth between little forms and large worlds.

If Christians can marvel at the mathematical order of nature, they can also marvel at the mathematical order discovered in language.

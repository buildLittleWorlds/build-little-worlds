# From the Book of Nature to the Book of Language

Christian thinkers have often spoken as if nature were a kind of book. The world is not mute chaos. It can be read because it is ordered. Mathematics becomes one of the ways human reason learns to read it.

Physics made this astonishing. Equations written in the language of mathematics describe falling bodies, planetary motion, electromagnetism, quantum fields, and the deep structure of matter. Eugene Wigner famously called this the "unreasonable effectiveness" of mathematics in the natural sciences.

Large language models invite a parallel wonder.

If physics taught us that matter is mathematically legible, LLMs suggest that language is mathematically legible too.

## The book of language

Language can seem too human, too historical, too contextual, and too alive to be mathematically patterned in any serious way. Words carry memory. Sentences depend on tone. Metaphors leap across domains. Arguments unfold in time. Jokes, prayers, stories, and confessions do not feel like the kind of things a matrix could help describe.

And yet LLMs work.

They work imperfectly, but not randomly. They work because written language contains structure: frequencies, relations, analogies, genres, semantic neighborhoods, contextual dependencies, and long-range expectations.

This does not mean language has been reduced to numbers. It means language leaves numerical traces.

## Creation, Logos, and intelligibility

For Christian theology, intelligibility is not an embarrassment. The world can be studied because it is not absurd at its root. Creation comes from the Logos, and human beings are creatures who can speak, reason, name, imagine, and discover.

LLMs do not replace that theology. They give it a new object of wonder.

The mathematical discoverability of language suggests that our words are not floating in a void. They belong to a world of relation. Meaning has neighborhoods. Context has force. Error has shape. Relevance can be weighed.

The book of nature and the book of language are not the same book. But both can be read because both belong to an ordered creation.

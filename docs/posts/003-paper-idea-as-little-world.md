# A Paper Idea as a Little World

This is a scaffold, not a finished paper proposal.

A paper idea is too easy to overstate. It can sound complete while hiding the sources, terms, and objections that would make it real. So this blog will treat a paper idea as a small world with boundaries.

A Rieff paper idea should include:

- **A narrow question:** one problem, not a whole intellectual biography.
- **A source base:** the Rieff texts and secondary sources that must be checked first.
- **A pressure point:** the tension that makes the question worth asking.
- **A test paragraph:** one paragraph that tries the argument at small scale.
- **A failure mode:** the most likely way the idea could become vague, derivative, or unsupported.

An example shape:

> How does Rieff's account of cultural authority change when read through the problem of therapeutic language rather than through a general story of secularization?

That is not yet an argument. It is a container for work. It says where citations need to be gathered, what terms need definition, and what sort of paragraph would count as progress.

The little world is not the final paper. It is the environment in which the paper can become possible.

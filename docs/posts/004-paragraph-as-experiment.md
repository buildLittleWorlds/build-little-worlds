# Paragraph as Experiment

This is a scaffold, not a settled interpretation.

A paragraph is where a primitive begins to become prose. It is also where vague understanding gets exposed. If a claim about Rieff cannot survive one clear paragraph, it probably is not ready to become an essay section.

The paragraph primitive has a simple form:

- Make one claim.
- Name the Rieff term or passage the claim depends on.
- State what the claim does not yet prove.
- Leave a note about the next citation needed.

Prototype paragraph:

> Rieff's importance for this project may lie less in any single doctrine than in the way his work treats culture as an ordering force that makes demands on persons. That claim needs careful qualification: it cannot be allowed to become a generic complaint about modernity, and it needs exact support from Rieff's own vocabulary. The next step is to anchor the sentence in a verified passage before building a larger interpretation around it.

The value of a paragraph like this is not polish. Its value is exposure. It shows where the claim has energy, where it leans on borrowed language, and where a real citation must enter before the thought grows.
